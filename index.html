Hi there, I'm Calvin Lai!

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow-In-Residence at the Edmond J. Safra Center for Ethics and the Department of Psychology at Harvard University. I received my Ph.D. in Social Psychology at the University of Virginia. My research interests include prejudice, stereotyping, attitudes, and implicit social cognition. study the unconscious mind. Thoughts or feelings outside of conscious awareness contribute to discrepancies between what people value (e.g., racial equality) and what people end up doing (e.g., racial discrimination). My research program employs large-scale comparative approaches to find the most effective approaches for changing these implicit biases. The breadth of this work has included: experimentally comparing seventeen interventions to reduce implicit racial biases, examining the role of moral emotions in reducing anti-gay prejudice, and a meta-analysis on change in implicit attitudes, stereotypes, self-esteem, and identity.ï»¿
